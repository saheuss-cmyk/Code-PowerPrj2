What is Implicit Bias

Implicit bias is the unconscious attitudes or associations individuals hold towards others, typically based on social groups. These biases can lead to preconceived notions or assumptions about others, rooted in stereotypes, and may unknowingly influence the way we perceive and interact with others.

Implicit Bias Tests

Implicit Bias Tests (IBTs) help individuals discover more about how their thoughts may be affected by implicit bias. Becoming aware of how unconscious associations shape one's perceptions of others is an important first step in treating others in a fair and equitable manner. Intersectionality

A closely related topic is intersectionality, which describes how different parts of one's identity, such as gender, race, sexual orientation, and more, intersect and shape one's experiences and treatment in society. Examining only one factor at a time is not enough to fully understand the complex ways in which power and inequality affect people's lives.
